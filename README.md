# SuperKart: Retail Sales Forecasting Using Machine Learning

Machine learning project for retail sales forecasting using historical sales and operational data to support inventory planning, pricing optimization, regional sales strategy, and data-driven business decision-making.

---

## Project Overview

This project develops machine learning models to forecast retail sales revenue for SuperKart outlets across multiple store categories and regions.

The analysis leverages historical sales and operational data to generate accurate sales forecasts that support inventory management, pricing strategies, supply chain planning, and business decision-making.

The project demonstrates how predictive analytics can help retail organizations improve operational efficiency and optimize revenue performance across their store network.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze historical retail sales and operational data
- Build machine learning models for sales forecasting
- Identify factors influencing outlet sales performance
- Improve inventory planning and regional sales strategies
- Support data-driven retail decision-making through predictive analytics

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains historical retail sales and operational data for SuperKart outlets across multiple cities and store categories.

The data includes product characteristics, outlet information, pricing data, product visibility, and historical sales revenue used to develop predictive sales forecasting models.

---

## Data Dictionary

The dataset includes:

- Product identifiers and categories
- Outlet identifiers and outlet characteristics
- Product weight and visibility
- Product MRP (pricing information)
- Outlet type, size, and location tier
- Historical product sales revenue

## Model Development

The project developed machine learning regression models to forecast outlet sales performance using historical sales and operational features.

Model evaluation focused on predictive accuracy and business interpretability to support operational forecasting and decision-making.

---

## Key Insights

- Sales performance is strongly influenced by product pricing (MRP), product categories, and store characteristics.

- Categories such as Fruits & Vegetables and Snack Foods contribute significantly to overall revenue performance.

- Store-related factors such as outlet type, store size, and city tier play an important role in sales generation.

- Supermarket Type 2 outlets, medium-sized stores, and Tier 2 cities emerged as key revenue drivers.

- Revenue generation is concentrated among a limited number of high-performing products and outlets, creating opportunities for strategic optimization and scaling.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Prioritize inventory allocation for high-performing product categories and store types to reduce stock shortages and maximize revenue.

- Develop tier-based regional sales strategies with stronger operational focus on Tier 2 cities.

- Optimize pricing strategies and promotional campaigns using the strong relationship between MRP and sales performance.

- Align shelf space allocation with high-performing products to improve sales efficiency and retail profitability.

- Integrate the forecasting model into operational planning workflows to support inventory management, supply chain optimization, and business decision-making.

- Continuously monitor and retrain forecasting models using updated sales data to maintain predictive performance.
